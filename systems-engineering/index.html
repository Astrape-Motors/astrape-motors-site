WHERE THE STANDARD COMES FROM

ISO/IEC JTC 1/SC 7.

The international subcommittee for Software and systems engineering — the group that writes the life-cycle, architecture and quality standards the whole field runs on.

ISO committee page →

Formed in 1987 by merging ISO/TC 97 and IEC/TC 83, the subcommittee was renamed from Software Engineering to Software and Systems Engineering in 2000 — the moment "systems" became the point. Its scope: the processes, supporting tools and technologies for engineering software products and systems — spanning enterprise architecture, the engineering environment, the bodies of knowledge, and IT asset management.

The discipline's agreed map of what systems engineering knows — the ground the standards below stand on. Maintained by INCOSE, the IEEE Systems Council and Stevens Institute; the six documents that follow are how we put it to work on hardware.
