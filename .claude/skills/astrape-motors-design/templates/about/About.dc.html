HOW WE THINK

It's cybernetics, applied to hardware.

Every machine we work on is a loop: sense, compute, actuate — measure the true state, decide what should happen, and make it move. Systems engineering is the discipline that keeps that loop honest end to end, so a platform behaves like one machine instead of four subsystems that don't talk.

We specialize in making things move with electric motors and software — and we take the hard problems no one else will. We work at the systems level — architecture, integration, controls and V&V; when a job needs stamped detail design, we bring in the right PE. Every stage produces something you can measure, not a slide deck.

THE NAME

Lightning, and the art of steering.

The company is two old Greek ideas welded together: the flash that moves, and the hand that steers it.

ἀστραπή
ASTRAPĒ · LIGHTNING

The flash of the bolt — the fire Zeus throws. The one thing in the old world that was pure power and pure signal at once. It's our mark, and our name.

κυβερνήτης
KYBERNĒTĒS · THE STEERSMAN

The helmsman who reads the sea and corrects the tiller. Norbert Wiener took the word in 1948 to name cybernetics. The same root, through Latin, becomes governor — the office, and the feedback device that holds an engine's speed.

The dream is older than the word. Hephaestus forged Talos, the bronze automaton that walked the shores of Crete on its own — the first machine built to move itself. We're still at it. We just measure them now.

“We have decided to call the entire field of control and communication theory Cybernetics, from the Greek κυβερνήτης, or steersman.”

— NORBERT WIENER · CYBERNETICS · 1948
