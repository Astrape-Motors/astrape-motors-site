MARINE HYBRID PROPULSION · 2025

A hybrid drive that holds station in a current.

We took a workboat builder's diesel platform and closed the loop — electric propulsion, a supervisory controller, and the validation rig to prove it before it ever touched water.

THE PROBLEM

Diesel couldn't hold position precisely enough — and burned fuel trying.

The builder needed dynamic positioning for survey work in tidal currents. The existing mechanical drivetrain was too coarse and too thirsty at low speed. They had the hull and the mission; they needed the system that made it behave.

THE APPROACH

Close the loop: sense → compute → actuate.

SENSE

Position & load

GNSS, IMU and shaft-load instrumentation to read true vessel state and current at the hull.

COMPUTE

Supervisory control

Model-based DP controller and power arbitration, code-generated and calibrated against measured data.

ACTUATE

Electric drive

Hybrid electric propulsion with fine torque control — proven on a HIL rig before sea trials.
