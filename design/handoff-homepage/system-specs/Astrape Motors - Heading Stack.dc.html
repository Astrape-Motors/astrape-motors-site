The gradient is emphasis — it has a level.

Rule of thumb: one gradient per view, on the most important heading. At the top it's the brand pair (blue → violet). When a heading names a discipline, it drops to that discipline's key color. Everything below stays solid so the effect keeps its weight.

THE L3 TEST

Apply the gradient only if both are true: the heading is the single subject of its view, and it's the only gradient on screen. Pass → colored by the subject (brand pair at L1; the discipline's key at L3). Fail → it falls back to what you like: solid (L4) with the key in the eyebrow or bar (L2). That's the whole reason the capabilities grid stays solid but a dedicated Controls page earns the clay gradient.
